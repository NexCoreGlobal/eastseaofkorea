The Facts

1

Historical Right

For over 2,000 years, this sea has been called the "East Sea" in Korean records, maps, and official documents. This name reflects Korea's historical claim to these waters.

2

International Law

According to international maritime law, the naming rights of territorial waters belong to the coastal nation. Korea, as the eastern coastal nation, has the rightful authority to name these waters.

3

Japan's Lobbying

During the Japanese occupation (1910-1945) and through subsequent diplomatic pressure, Japan unilaterally changed the name to "Sea of Japan." This was done without Korea's consent or agreement.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why is this important?

A: The name of a territory reflects sovereignty and historical rights. Reclaiming "East Sea" is about asserting Korea's rightful authority over its own waters and correcting a historical injustice.

Q: How long has this dispute existed?

A: The deliberate name change to "Sea of Japan" occurred during Japanese occupation. Since Korea's independence in 1945, this has remained a point of international dispute.

Q: What do international organizations say?

A: Most international bodies recognize both names due to conflicting claims, but this should not be the case. Korea's historical and legal right to name its own waters is clear.

Q: How can I help?

A: Sign our petition, share on social media, support the movement, and educate others about Korea's rightful claim to the East Sea.
